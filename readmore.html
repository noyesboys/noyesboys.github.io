Complete Terpene Guide

Explore terpene profiles, aromas, effects and strain combinations

What Are Terpenes?

Terpenes are aromatic compounds found in many plants, including cannabis. They're responsible for the distinct smells and flavors of different strains, but they do much more than just provide aroma. Research suggests terpenes work synergistically with cannabinoids like THC and CBD to create the "entourage effect" - influencing the overall experience and effects.

Common Cannabis Terpenes

Myrcene

Aroma: Earthy, musky, herbal

Effects: Sedating, relaxing, muscle relaxant

Found In: Noyes Breath, many Indicas, mangoes, hops

Limonene

Aroma: Citrus, lemon, orange

Effects: Uplifting, stress relief, mood enhancement

Found In: Purple Rainbow, citrus fruits, rosemary

Pinene

Aroma: Pine, fresh, woody

Effects: Alertness, memory retention, counteracts THC impairment

Found In: White Noyes, pine needles, rosemary

Caryophyllene

Aroma: Spicy, peppery, woody

Effects: Anti-inflammatory, pain relief, stress reduction

Found In: Many Noyes strains, black pepper, cloves

Linalool

Aroma: Floral, lavender, sweet

Effects: Calming, anti-anxiety, sleep aid

Found In: Purple strains, lavender, birch bark

Maximizing Terpenes in Your Grow

  • Temperature Control: Keep flowering temps between 65-75°F to preserve volatile terpenes
  • Proper Curing: Slow dry and cure in glass jars to maintain terpene profiles
  • Harvest Timing: Terpene production peaks just before full trichome maturation
  • Genetics Matter: Choose strains known for strong terpene production
  • Organic Growing: Living soil can enhance terpene development

Terpenes in Noyes Boys Strains

All our genetics are selected for robust terpene production. Our breeding program focuses on preserving and enhancing the aromatic compounds that make each strain unique. From the tropical mango notes in Mango Aliens to the floral complexity of Purple Rainbow, every Noyes Boys strain offers a rich terpene experience.

The Complete Guide to Feminized Cannabis Seeds

December 15, 2024 Mr. Noyes Feminized Seeds 12 min read

After 15 years in the cannabis breeding game, I've witnessed the complete transformation of how we approach cannabis cultivation. When I started, finding quality feminized seeds was like searching for a needle in a haystack. Today, they represent over 90% of the home cultivation market, and there's good reason for that dominance.

What Are Feminized Seeds?

Feminized seeds are bred to eliminate male chromosomes, ensuring that approximately 99.9% of plants will be female. This matters because only female plants produce the resinous buds we're after. Male plants are primarily useful for breeding programs - something most home growers want to avoid.

The Science Behind Feminization

The feminization process involves treating female plants with colloidal silver or silver thiosulfate solution (STS). This treatment blocks ethylene production, forcing the female plant to produce pollen sacs instead of pistils. The pollen from this "reversed" female contains only X chromosomes, guaranteeing female offspring when used to pollinate another female.

At Noyes Boys, we use the STS method exclusively. It's more reliable than colloidal silver and produces more stable genetics across generations. Our feminization process takes place in a completely isolated environment to prevent contamination.

Why Choose Feminized Seeds?

  • Space Efficiency: No need to grow extra plants to account for males you'll remove
  • Time Savings: No sexing period or plant removal disrupting your garden
  • Guaranteed Returns: Every seed becomes a productive plant
  • Consistent Results: Predictable plant characteristics and flowering times
  • Legal Compliance: Stay within plant count limits more easily

Quality Control at Noyes Boys

Our feminization process involves multiple quality checkpoints. We test every batch for:

  • Germination rates (minimum 85%)
  • Female rates (minimum 99.5%)
  • Hermaphrodite tendencies (less than 0.1%)
  • Genetic stability across 3 generations

Each batch is grown out by our test team before release. We document growth patterns, yields, cannabinoid profiles, and any stability issues. Only genetics that meet our strict standards make it to market.

Growing Tips for Feminized Seeds

Feminized seeds respond best to stable environmental conditions. Stress from light leaks, temperature fluctuations, or pH swings can trigger hermaphrodite traits in some genetics. Here's how to maximize success:

  • Light Discipline: Maintain strict 18-6 during veg, complete darkness during flowering
  • Temperature Stability: Keep temperatures between 70-80°F, avoid swings greater than 10°F
  • pH Management: Maintain 6.0-7.0 in soil, 5.5-6.5 in hydro
  • Gentle Handling: Avoid high-stress training until plants are well established
  • Quality Nutrients: Use balanced feeding programs, avoid nutrient burn

Common Misconceptions

Myth: "Feminized seeds are more likely to hermaphrodite."

Truth: Quality feminized seeds from reputable breeders show no higher hermaphrodite rates than regular seeds. Poor genetics or environmental stress cause hermaphrodites, not the feminization process itself.

Myth: "You can't breed with feminized seeds."

Truth: Feminized plants can absolutely be used for breeding. You can reverse them to create pollen or use them as mothers in regular breeding programs. Some of our best genetics started as feminized selections.

Myth: "Feminized seeds produce lower yields."

Truth: Yield depends entirely on genetics and growing conditions, not whether seeds are feminized or regular. Many of our highest-yielding strains are feminized.

The Economics of Feminized Seeds

While feminized seeds cost more upfront, they're actually more economical for home growers:

  • No wasted space growing males you'll discard
  • Reduced electricity and nutrient costs
  • Guaranteed return on investment
  • No risk of accidental pollination destroying your crop

Choosing Quality Feminized Seeds

Not all feminized seeds are created equal. Look for breeders who:

  • Publish germination and female rates
  • Test genetics across multiple generations
  • Use proper feminization techniques (STS over colloidal silver)
  • Provide detailed growing information
  • Stand behind their products with guarantees

The Future of Feminized Genetics

Feminized seeds have become the foundation of modern cannabis cultivation. As legalization expands and home growing becomes more mainstream, the demand for reliable, high-quality feminized genetics will only increase.

At Noyes Boys, we're constantly improving our feminization techniques. We're working on tissue culture methods that could produce even more stable genetics, and exploring ways to preserve rare terpene profiles that sometimes get lost in traditional breeding.

The Bottom Line

Feminized seeds have revolutionized home cultivation by eliminating guesswork and maximizing garden efficiency. When sourced from reputable breeders who prioritize genetic stability, they offer the most reliable path to successful harvests.

Whether you're planning your first grow or your hundredth, feminized genetics provide the consistency and predictability that make great harvests possible. The science is sound, the benefits are clear, and the results speak for themselves.

Choose your genetics wisely, grow with care, and enjoy the fruits of this incredible plant.

My Experience Growing Purple Rainbow

Review by Alex Thompson - Intermediate Grower

After reading Mr. Noyes' descriptions of Purple Rainbow, I knew I had to try it. The promise of rainbow-colored trichomes and purple foliage seemed almost too good to be true. I'm happy to report it exceeded every expectation.

Germination & Early Growth (Week 1-3)

Seeds popped in 48 hours using the paper towel method. Emergence was uniform across all 4 seeds, with healthy white taproots. Seedlings showed hybrid vigor from day one - thick stems and broad leaves indicating the strong genetics.

By week 3, plants were showing the first hints of purple in the stems. Growing in 3-gallon fabric pots with living soil, they responded well to gentle feeding and 20/4 light schedule.

Vegetative Stage (Week 4-8)

This is where Purple Rainbow really shines. The growth rate was impressive - easily 2 inches per day under my 320W quantum board. Plants stayed relatively compact with tight node spacing, perfect for my 4x4 tent.

I started LST in week 5, and these plants responded beautifully. Very flexible branches that held their positions well. By week 8, I had perfect canopies ready for the flip.

Early Flower (Week 1-4 of 12/12)

The stretch was moderate - about 2x final height. Pistils appeared quickly, and the first hints of purple started showing in the fan leaves around week 3. The smell was already incredible - sweet berries with a hint of gas.

Mid to Late Flower (Week 5-9)

This is when the magic happens. By week 6, the entire plant was turning purple - not just leaves, but stems and even some of the bud structure. The trichomes started developing rainbow reflections that are impossible to capture in photos.

Feeding was straightforward - they handled full-strength bloom nutrients without any burn. I did notice they preferred slightly cooler temps (70-75°F) to really bring out the colors.

Harvest & Yield

Harvested at day 65 with about 20% amber trichomes. The colors were stunning - deep purple leaves with rainbow-tinted trichomes that sparkled under the lights. Final yield was 487g from 4 plants in the 4x4 tent.

Cure & Smoke Report

After a 4-week cure, the smell was intoxicating. Sweet berry notes with undertones of vanilla and gas. The smoke was smooth and flavorful, with effects hitting quickly.

Effects: Immediate euphoria followed by creative energy. Perfect for daytime use without paranoia or anxiety. Duration was about 3-4 hours with a gentle comedown.

Winter Growing in Canada: Beating the Cold Season Blues

December 12, 2024 Sarah Chen Seasonal Growing 7 min read

Let me be brutally honest - Canadian winters will test every ounce of your growing skills. After five years of basement cultivation through Maritime winters, I've learned that success comes down to three critical factors: humidity control, heat management, and light optimization.

The Humidity Challenge

Winter air in Canada can drop to 10-15% humidity indoors. Your plants need 40-60% during vegetation and 40-50% during flowering. Without proper humidification, you'll see stunted growth, crispy leaf edges, and increased susceptibility to pests like spider mites.

I use two humidifiers in my 4x8 grow room, plus wet towels hung near the intake fan. Monitor with multiple hygrometers - cheap ones lie, and humidity can vary 20% between different areas of your space.

Heat Management Without Breaking the Bank

Heating costs can double your electricity bill. LED lights help, but you still need supplemental heat. I installed a small space heater on a thermostat controller, maintaining 75-80°F during lights on, 65-70°F during lights off.

Insulation matters more than raw heating power. I wrapped my tent in moving blankets and sealed air leaks with weatherstripping. This simple upgrade cut my heating costs by 40%.

Light Optimization for Short Days

Winter depression affects plants too. Even with artificial lighting, shorter photoperiods can stress plants. I run 20/4 light cycles during winter vegetation instead of 18/6, giving plants extra energy to combat environmental stress.

Strain Selection for Winter Success

Some strains handle winter stress better than others. Indica-dominant varieties like Noyes Breath and White Noyes show better cold tolerance and humidity resilience than temperamental sativas.

The Bottom Line

Winter growing is absolutely doable, but it requires preparation and patience. Budget an extra $50-100 monthly for utilities, invest in quality environmental controls, and choose hardy genetics. The reward? Fresh homegrown while everyone else waits for spring.
